01

The 8 resolutions

Resolution 01 — Core tension

The brand is a fast gifting utility that feels elevated on arrival

The tension between "speed-first conversion engine" and "handcrafted elegance" is not a contradiction — it is a sequence. The experience of ordering is fast and frictionless. The experience of receiving is warm and beautiful. Both are true. Mode assigns which register is active.

Before "Not a luxury brand" (System) vs. "handcrafted elegance, artisanal detail" (Kit) — two documents pulling in opposite directions.
Resolved Ordering experience = speed + clarity. Receiving experience = warmth + presentation. Assign each to a brand mode. Both statements are now true.

Resolution 02 — Color roles

One CTA color. One brand warmth color. No overlap.

Before Primary Pink (#F58BA6) AND Rosewood (#D16681) both assigned to CTA/button roles across two documents.
Resolved Rosewood #D16681 = all action/urgency triggers, buttons, CTAs.
Primary Pink #F58BA6 = brand identity, emotional warmth, highlights only.

Resolution 03 — Audience

Primary: busy professionals 22–40 who need a fast, beautiful gift. Secondary: men buying surprises.

Before System: "Men + women, urgent gifters." Kit: "Women 22–40, party planners." No reconciliation.
Resolved Primary: women 22–40, planned + urgent gifters. Secondary: men, primarily surprise/romantic orders. Same funnel, different CTA angles per campaign.

Resolution 04 — Brand modes mapped to tone

Three modes. Three tones. One table. No guesswork.

Every piece of content — post, message, insert, script — is assigned a mode before it's written.

Brand mode Tone mode Platform Example language
Express Urgency / Sales Instagram, WhatsApp "Pick. Pay. Done."
Celebration Emotion / Warmth Instagram, packaging "Make someone smile."
Retention Soft / Relational WhatsApp follow-up "Hope they loved it."

Resolution 05 — Typography scope

Merienda: gift tags and note cards only. Full stop.

Before System: "gift tags, <5%." Kit: "packaging taglines, event labels" — quiet scope creep that compounds.
Resolved Merienda = physical gift tags + handwritten note card text only. All packaging taglines use Mulish Medium. No exceptions.

Resolution 06 — Archetype in kit

Operator × Giver now expressed in both documents.

Before Archetype "Operator × Giver" appears only in the System. Kit has zero reference to it.
Resolved Operator = the speed, the system, the frictionless flow. Giver = the warmth, the box, the note. Operator drives ordering UX. Giver drives packaging + gift-moment content.

Resolution 07 — WhatsApp scripts

5 core scripts built. See Section 4.

Order confirmation, delivery update, photo prompt, 7-day retention, and corporate/bulk inquiry. Each tagged to a brand mode and reviewed against the pre-output checklist.

Resolution 08 — Content calendar

3 posts/week skeleton. Locked cadence, zero decision fatigue.

Week structure: Monday (Offer/Express), Wednesday (Gift Moment/Celebration), Friday (Proof/Retention). WhatsApp follow-ups on Day 0 and Day 7 post-delivery. See Section 5.

03

Voice & tone system

Express

Urgency · Sales · Action

"Don't overthink it. Just send cake."

  • Short sentences, single idea
  • Lead with the action, not the product
  • Price and CTA always visible
  • No decorative language
  • Max 2 lines before the CTA
Celebration

Emotion · Warmth · Gift context

"Every box feels like a celebration."

  • Reference the recipient, not the product
  • Situational ("birthday," "just because")
  • Warm but never sentimental
  • Slightly longer — still ends in action
  • Use Cake-Card Wedge visuals
Retention

Soft · Relational · Re-order

"Hope they loved it. Send another?"

  • Acknowledge the completed gift
  • Low pressure, high warmth
  • Mention the discount naturally
  • One action only — the re-order link
  • Timing: Day 0 + Day 7 post-delivery

Playfair Display — headlines & wordmark

Hero headlines, product names, section titles. Bold weight only. Never italic in UI contexts.

Mulish — all body copy, taglines, packaging taglines, UI

Physical applications only. Under 5% total usage. Never in digital UI, never on packaging taglines, never in social posts.

06

Pre-output checklist

Every asset — post, script, insert, UI card — must pass all 6 before publishing. Click to check off.

Can someone order immediately? A clear path to purchase exists within this asset.
Is it clear in 3 seconds? A stranger understands what is being sold without explanation.
Is the price visible? No price = no card. Non-negotiable.
Is there exactly one CTA? One action. Not two. Not "DM or call or visit."
Does it reduce thinking? No unnecessary choices or ambiguity added.
Does it trigger emotion? Shows a gift moment, a face, or a feeling — not just a product shot.
