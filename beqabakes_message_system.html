00Core principles

What this system is built to do

The original message kit had the right instincts — speed, payment links, follow-up discipline — but introduced parallel systems that contradicted the locked stack. Every conflict has been resolved. This document is the corrected, canonical version.

Operating principle: You are not "chatting with customers." You are re-triggering gifting behaviour. Every message exists to move someone one step closer to a confirmed order — or to a confirmed repeat order. If a message doesn't do that, it doesn't send.

What changed from the original

Follow-up timing — unified

Day 3–5 / 7–10 / 14–21 sequence retired. Locked to Day 0 (Script 03) + Day 7 (Script 04) only. Phase 2 three-beat sequence unlocks at Month 3 review if data supports it.

SKU count — reduced to 3

Five Quick Order items confirmed as the fast-lane execution layer — distinct from the three canonical offer tiers. The Quick Order menu is the express checkout; the Lookbook covers the full catalogue. Both architectures coexist. Product names below are canonical and must be used identically across all messages, posts, and Paystack links.

Scripts — no duplicates

Confirmation, delivery update, photo prompt, and pending payment scripts are removed from this document. Canonical versions live in Platform OS Scripts 01–06. This document adds entry flow and broadcast templates only.

Brand mode — tagged throughout

Every template in this document carries a mode tag. Emoji count reduced to one maximum per message — warmth comes from words, not decoration.

Upsell — moved to post-payment

Add-on offer fires after Script 01 payment confirmation — never before. Pre-payment upsells create friction at the highest-value moment.

CRM — one system

Nine WA labels collapsed to four, mapped directly to the Notion CRM buyer types. WhatsApp labels are operational shortcuts; Notion is the record of truth.

02Entry system

Every new WhatsApp contact sees the greeting message first. It presents three options only — Quick Order, bigger cake conversation, or a question. No custom cake path. No six-option menu. Mode: Express.

03Order flow

Menu selection → details → payment → confirmation.

The order flow is the conversion engine. Every step moves toward a confirmed payment. No freestyle. No delays. No "I'll pay later." The flow is identical regardless of which product is selected — only the payment link changes.

Closing logic: Remove thinking → show options → ask "which one?" → send payment link immediately. Don't wait for a "yes" before sending the link. The link is the commitment device.

05Upsell system

Add-ons only after payment is confirmed.

The upsell offer fires immediately after Script 01 — never before payment. Pre-payment upsells introduce friction at the moment of highest intent. Post-payment upsells feel like service, not sales.

ExpressUpsell offerTrigger: immediately after Script 01
Want to make it extra special? 🎀 Ribbon packaging — ₦1,000 💌 Extra note card — ₦1,000 🕯 Candles — ₦500 Reply with what you'd like to add and we'll update your order.
Emojis here are functional indicators, not decoration. One reply accepted. Do not re-send if no response.
Upsell timing law: This fires after Script 01. Never before the summary script. Never during the details collection. The order is confirmed — now we're enhancing it. If the customer doesn't reply to the upsell, the order proceeds as confirmed. No follow-up on the upsell.
06Retention sequence

Two beats. Locked. Day 0 and Day 7.

The retention sequence is owned by COS v1 and referenced here. Two beats only — until data from the Month 3 review shows that adding a third beat increases repeat rate. The sequence fires after every delivery without exception.

Day 0 — delivery

Script 03 fires

Photo prompt + ₦500 reshare incentive. Tag photo in Notion Proof bank when received.

If reshare

Script 06 fires

Referral prompt to resharing buyer. Zero-cost new lead acquisition.

Day 7 — re-order

Script 04 fires

BACK500 code + direct Paystack link. Retention mode — soft, one action.

If purchase

Loop restarts

New order → new Day 0 sequence. Update Notion buyer type to Repeat or VIP.

Timing is non-negotiable. Day 7 means Day 7. Not Day 6, not Day 8. The BACK500 code creates a predictable incentive rhythm. If buyers know it arrives on Day 7, they start anticipating it — which is the mechanism that builds habitual re-ordering.

Day 7 — rotate product by buyer history

Script 04 includes a direct Paystack link. Rotate the featured product based on what the buyer ordered last — show them something adjacent, not identical:

07Reactivation — Phase 2

The three-beat sequence. Gated until Month 3 data supports it.

The original message system's Day 3–5 / Day 7–10 / Day 14–21 sequence is preserved here as Phase 2. It activates only when: the two-beat Day 0 / Day 7 sequence has run for at least 6 weeks and the Month 3 data review shows that the Day 7 repeat rate is below 15%. Until both conditions are met, this section is planning material only.

Phase 2 gate: Do not activate until the Month 3 review. Running three follow-up beats before the two-beat sequence is proven creates noise, raises spam risk, and makes the data unreadable. The simpler system runs first.

08CRM labels

Rule: Every label change in WhatsApp Business triggers a corresponding update in the Notion CRM buyer database. The two systems must stay in sync. When in doubt, update Notion first.

09Broadcast templates

Weekly push. Broadcast lists only. Never groups.

Broadcasts go to buyers who have previously interacted or ordered — never to cold contacts. Segment by WA label before sending. Two to three per week maximum. Every broadcast carries a direct Paystack link.

Broadcast rule: Send to WhatsApp broadcast lists, never to groups. Group sends expose all members' numbers and feel impersonal. Broadcast lists deliver individually and respect privacy. Segment by label — "Paid" and "Follow-up" buyers get different messages.

10Non-negotiable rules

What never changes. Regardless of volume or situation.

Always do

  • Reply within 10 minutes during 9AM–7PM. Speed is the first trust signal.
  • Show price in every product mention. No hidden pricing, ever.
  • Send the payment link immediately after order summary. Don't wait for verbal confirmation.
  • Use quick replies for standard flows. Don't freestyle the order conversation.
  • Tag every buyer in Notion after each order. CRM discipline = retention system working.
  • Send Script 03 after every delivery. No exceptions. The Proof bank depends on this.

Never do

  • Never ask "What do you want?" Always guide with numbered options.
  • Never delay the payment link. "I'll pay later" is a lost order. Send the link and close.
  • Never send the upsell before payment. After Script 01 only.
  • Never send the pending reminder twice. Once is service. Twice is pressure.
  • Never broadcast to cold contacts. Broadcast only to buyers who have ordered or interacted.
  • Never use product names not in the canonical list. The five Quick Order names are: Quick Birthday Cake, Send Love (Bento), Mini Celebration Box, Just Because Cake, Cupcake Box. Use these exactly — no abbreviations, no invented names.

Daily execution priorities

Priority 1 — always first

Reply to all open WA conversations. Within 10 minutes of receiving during business hours.

Priority 2 — script fires

Check Notion for any Day 7 triggers due today. Send Script 04 to qualifying buyers with direct link.

Priority 3 — broadcast day

Mon/Wed/Fri: send broadcast to segmented list. Proof push on Friday uses the Proof bank photo of the week.
