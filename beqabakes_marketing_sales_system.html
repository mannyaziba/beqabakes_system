01 Market reality

Who we sell to. What stops them. What moves them.

This section is the intelligence layer. It defines the audience, maps the psychological journey from unaware to order-placed, and names every objection and core tension. Everything downstream — campaigns, scripts, content — is a direct response to this section.

Market truth

People are not buying cake. They are buying fast emotional delivery.

Audience definition (locked — Brand v2 owns this)

Primary audience

Women 22–40, Abuja

Planned and urgent gifters. Birthday planners, party organisers, romantic gesture buyers. Instagram-present, aesthetics-aware, time-constrained.

Triggers: "It's their birthday today" / "I need something beautiful fast"

Secondary audience

Men 25–45, Abuja

Romantic surprise buyers. Typically last-minute, high-urgency, lower product knowledge. Need reassurance more than options.

Triggers: "I forgot our anniversary" / "I need something today"

Not our customer: Highly custom, complex cake buyers requiring 3+ days lead time, detailed consultation, or multi-tier structural builds. They are not wrong — they are just a different business. Redirect them warmly.

Core tensions

Problem

Need a meaningful gift quickly

Pain

Time pressure + decision stress

Desire

Make someone feel genuinely special

Fear

It won't arrive on time or look good

Frustration

Too many options, slow vendors

False belief

"Good gifts take time to plan"

Objection map

Objection

Root cause

Removal strategy

"Will it be nice?" Trust gap — new vendor Delivery photos in every post. Proof bank on Friday feed. Never show product without context.
"How fast can it come?" Time sensitivity "Ready today" + same-day messaging on every Monday Express post and WA broadcast.
"How much does it cost?" Hidden pricing fear Price always visible on posts and in WhatsApp. No price = no card rule is absolute.
"Can I trust you?" Vendor uncertainty Testimonials, delivery photos, packaging visuals. Friday proof post closes this consistently.
"Is ordering stressful?" Past bad experience "No stress" messaging in Express mode. Pre-built bundles eliminate choice paralysis.
02 Offer system

Three tiers. One decision logic.

The offer stack is not a menu. It is a funnel. Each tier serves a specific buyer state and purchase intent. Content is not a product tier — it is a distribution mechanism that feeds all three tiers.

Entry tier

Express Bento

For urgent, last-minute gifters. Pre-built. No decisions. Same-day delivery. The fastest path from "I need a gift" to "done."

Mode: Express

Core tier

Signature Cakes

Primary revenue SKU. Planned and semi-planned buyers. Emotional gifting with visual impact. Higher price point, higher attachment.

Mode: Celebration

Premium tier

Gift Sets

Upsell to highest AOV. Full experience purchase. Corporate-adjacent. Planned gifters who want to send the most complete gift possible.

Mode: Celebration Corporate

Content is not a tier. Instagram posts are a distribution mechanism, not a "Free" product. Framing content as a tier trains the wrong mental model — it suggests leads need to be nurtured up a product ladder, when what actually happens is simpler: see post → feel urgency → WhatsApp → order.

Value equation

Dream outcome

Make someone feel celebrated instantly — without any stress

Likelihood of success

High — delivery photos, testimonials, and packaging proof are visible at every stage

Time delay

Minimal — same-day delivery is the core promise. "Send a cake in minutes" is the system line.

Effort required

Near zero — WhatsApp quick order, pre-built options, one tap from post to conversation

Offer framing rule

This is NOT a complex cake ordering process.
This IS a fast, simple gifting solution for people who need a gift today — without thinking.

If a prospect asks "can you do a five-tier fondant display piece for 200 guests?" the answer is: warmly redirect. That is not our business. Protecting the system protects the speed promise.

06 Conversion engine

The WhatsApp chat that closes every order.

Conversion happens in WhatsApp. The chat structure below is the conceptual scaffolding. Each step maps to a specific COS v1 script or action. The scripts are the operational layer — this is the strategic logic behind them.

Closing logic: Remove thinking → show options → ask "which one?" Every extra question, every extra decision, every unclear price is a conversion that doesn't happen.

07 CRM system

A real CRM. Not a mental one.

The original MSOS described a "Mental CRM" — buyer tags held in memory. This is replaced with a defined Notion schema. Five fields, one database, tagged after every order. The retention and growth systems depend on this.

"Mental CRM" is retired. Once weekly order volume exceeds 10, memory-based tagging fails and the Day 7 nudge, VIP identification, and referral logic all break down. The Notion schema below takes 20 minutes to build and runs indefinitely.

08 Campaign architecture

Four beats. Every campaign runs this structure.

The original MSOS had a 3-beat campaign structure (Pre / Launch / Post). The fourth beat — Retention — has been added. Every campaign that ends at "Post" leaves the repeat purchase loop disconnected. Retention closes it.

Celebration Beat 1 — Pre-launch (tease)
Goal Build anticipation. Prime the audience's gifting intent before the offer appears.
Content Gift-moment Stories, "something's coming" teaser posts, behind-the-scenes baking Reel.
Duration 2–3 days before launch day
Express Beat 2 — Launch (offer + urgency)
Goal Drive immediate orders. All friction removed. Single CTA to WhatsApp.
Content Monday Express post + WhatsApp broadcast. Price visible. "Ready today" stated. Rosewood CTA.
Duration Launch day + 24 hours
Retention Beat 3 — Post (proof + social)
Goal Capture proof from campaign orders. Build trust for the next campaign cycle.
Content Friday proof post from deliveries. Script 03 photo prompt sent to all campaign buyers.
Duration Day 3–7 post-launch
Retention Beat 4 — Retention (re-order push)
Goal Convert campaign buyers into repeat customers. This is the revenue multiplier beat.
Content Script 04 (Day 7 re-order nudge) to all campaign buyers. BACK500 code. Soft, relational tone.
Duration Day 7 exactly post-delivery
Campaign calendar note: Monthly themes from COS v1 anchor each campaign — Month 1 = "No-stress gifting" (Express Bento focus), Month 2 = "Make someone smile" (Gift Sets), Month 3 = "Last-minute saves" (same-day relaunch). Each campaign runs all four beats.
09 Growth system

Three loops. Three expansion paths. All gated.

Growth happens through compounding loops, not one-time pushes. Each loop feeds the next. Expansion into new channels and business models is gated behind defined thresholds — so nothing activates before the foundation is proven.

The three core loops

Content loop

Post ↓ Order placed ↓ Delivery + photo ↓ Friday proof post ↓ more orders

Referral loop

Buyer reshares ↓ Referral prompt fires ↓ Friend sees → WA link ↓ New customer enters ↓ zero-cost acquisition

Retention loop

Order confirmed ↓ Day 7 nudge sent ↓ BACK500 redeemed ↓ Repeat order placed ↓ AOV compounds

10 Tool stack

Six tools. Each with a single, defined role.

The original tool stack listed four tools and omitted delivery tracking and proof-bank management. The updated stack adds two — and defines what each tool does and does not do, to prevent role-bleed.

WA

WhatsApp Business

Primary sales channel. All order conversations, scripts 01–05, Day 0 + Day 7 nurture. CRM tagging trigger.

IG

Instagram

Discovery + desire. 3 posts/week, daily Stories, Reels. Feeds WhatsApp with warm leads. Not a sales channel.

Pa

Paystack

Payment processing only. Link sent in WhatsApp after selection confirmed. No other role in the flow.

No

Notion

CRM database + Proof bank + Content brief archive + Campaign pipeline tracker + Weekly metrics log.

Rd

Rider / Delivery

Delivery confirmation trigger. When rider marks delivered → Scripts 02 + 03 fire. Tracked in Notion CRM.

Dr

Google Drive

Approved visual asset library. Raw shoot files + final post images. Brand assets + Lookbook PDF storage.

Future tool: WhatsApp automation (Wati or Interakt) activates when order volume makes manual script-sending inefficient — estimated at 15+ orders/week. Until then, scripts are sent manually. The infrastructure is identical; automation is a volume decision, not a capability decision.
11 Control system

Pre-publish and post-delivery quality gates.

Two checklists govern every output — one before it goes out, one after it lands. The pre-publish checklist is owned by COS v1 (referenced here). The post-delivery checklist is owned by the MSOS — it governs operational completeness, not content quality.

Pre-publish (owned by COS v1)

  • Can someone order immediately from this?
  • Is the price visible?
  • Is it clear in under 3 seconds?
  • Is there exactly one CTA?
  • Does it reduce thinking — not add it?
  • Does it trigger emotion or urgency?

6 of 6 required. See COS v1 for full detail.

Post-delivery (owned by MSOS)

  • Order confirmed and logged in Notion CRM?
  • Buyer type tagged (New / Repeat / VIP)?
  • Delivery confirmed by rider?
  • Script 03 (photo prompt) sent — Day 0?
  • Script 04 (re-order nudge) scheduled — Day 7?
  • Proof received? Tagged in Proof bank?

6 of 6 required per order. No exceptions.

Operating principles (5 laws)

Law 1

Speed wins sales

Faster response, faster ordering, faster delivery beats prettier every time.

Law 2

Simplicity drives conversion

Every extra choice, every extra step, every unclear price is a lost order.

Law 3

Emotion drives action

Logic justifies. Emotion decides. Every post leads with a feeling, not a feature.

Law 4

Proof builds trust

One real delivery photo is worth ten designed posts. The Proof bank is the most valuable asset in this system.

Law 5

Follow-up creates repeat

The Day 7 nudge is not optional. Every uncontacted past buyer is a missed re-order that costs nothing to prevent.
