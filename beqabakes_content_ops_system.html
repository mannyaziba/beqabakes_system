00 System overview

What this system does

The COS turns Beqabakes' brand architecture into a repeatable production engine. Every output — post, story, WhatsApp message, packaging insert — flows from the same logic and ends at the same place: an order.

Operating principle: Replace "What should I post?" with "What gifting situation am I solving today?" Every piece of content starts with a situation, not a format.

Core flow (locked)

Brand System v2 → Mode (Express / Celebration / Retention) → SIA+ brief → Visual (Cake-Card Wedge) → Platform adapters → Order via WhatsApp → Repeat loop

System purpose

Orders (conversion)

Every post has a direct path to a WhatsApp order. No orphaned content.

WhatsApp conversations (entry)

Instagram drives intent. WhatsApp closes it. Both are primary.

Repeat purchases (retention)

Day 7 nudge + packaging insert create a passive re-order engine.

Social proof (trust)

Active proof collection means Friday posts always have real material.

System law: If a piece of content cannot lead to an immediate order, it does not get published. This is not a guideline. It is the filter for every output.
01 Brand modes

The three registers

Every piece of content — post, message, insert, script — is assigned a mode before it is written. No exceptions. Mode determines tone, structure, CTA type, and visual approach.

Rule: Content modes are Express, Celebration, and Retention. The old Offer / Emotion / Proof naming is retired. All documents and Notion databases use the new terms.
Express

Urgency & action

Sales · Offer · Conversion

"Pick. Pay. Done."

  • Lead with action, not product
  • Price always visible
  • Max 2 lines before CTA
  • Single CTA — WhatsApp order link
  • Rosewood (#D16681) CTA button
  • No decorative language
Celebration

Warmth & gift moment

Emotion · Gift context · Desire

"Every box feels like a celebration."

  • Reference recipient, not product
  • Situational (birthday, just because)
  • Warm but never sentimental
  • Slightly longer — still ends in action
  • Cake-Card Wedge mandatory
  • Pink (#F58BA6) accent, not CTA
Retention

Soft & relational

Re-order · Follow-up · Loyalty

"Hope they loved it. Send another?"

  • Acknowledge the completed gift
  • Low pressure, high warmth
  • Mention discount naturally
  • One action — re-order link only
  • Timing: Day 0 + Day 7 post-delivery
  • Blush palette, Mulish 400

Mode decision guide

Ask these three questions in order before writing anything:

Is this primarily driving an order right now?ExpressNext question
Does it show a gift moment, reaction, or emotion?CelebrationRethink — don't publish

03 Pre-output checklist

6 of 6 required before publishing

Every asset — post, script, insert, UI card — must pass all six. If any fails, fix the content, not the checklist. This is the brand's quality gate.

  • Can someone order immediately from this?
    A clear path to purchase exists within this asset. One tap to WhatsApp.
  • Is the price visible?
    No price = no card, no post, no message. Non-negotiable regardless of mode.
  • Is it clear in under 3 seconds?
    A stranger who has never heard of Beqabakes understands what is being sold.
  • Is there exactly one CTA?
    One action. Not two. Not "DM or call or visit." One link, one direction.
  • Does it reduce thinking — not add it?
    No unnecessary choices, no ambiguity, no "which one do I pick?" moment.
  • Does it trigger emotion or urgency?
    Shows a gift moment, a face, or a feeling — not just a product on a table.
If the answer is No to any item: fix the content before the checklist. The checklist does not flex. The content does.
04 Content stack

Four layers. Each with a revenue target.

The stack defines what gets produced at each level of abstraction — from thinking to distribution to conversion. Every layer has a measurable threshold so you know if it's working.

Thinking One SIA+ brief per post slot (3/week). Mode assigned before any copy or visual work begins. This is the non-negotiable first step. No brief = no post. 3 briefs / week
Authority One longer piece per month derived from the month's theme. "How to send a birthday cake in Abuja in under an hour." Repurposed into Reels, captions, and Stories. One brief feeds five distribution units. 1 piece / month
Distribution 3 posts + daily Stories per week. Instagram = discovery and desire. WhatsApp = conversion and retention. Both channels are primary — neither is secondary. 3 posts + daily stories
Conversion Minimum 2 WhatsApp order initiations per post. If a post consistently falls below this, the brief or timing is reviewed — not just the design. Content that doesn't convert is content that doesn't get repeated. 2+ WA orders / post

Content derivation — one SIA+ unit → many outputs

The goal is maximum output from minimum brief-writing effort. Each SIA+ should yield at least four usable pieces:

Long-form (monthly authority piece)

"How to send a birthday cake in Abuja in under 1 hour" — turns into Reel script + caption + Story sequence + WhatsApp broadcast.

Distillations (3 posts from 1 brief)

"Forgot a birthday?" → "Send this today" → "No stress gifting." Same situation, three different angles, three post slots filled.

Micro insights (captions)

"People don't want cake — they want the moment." Pull one principle from the decision logic field and lead with it.

Proof opportunities

Every delivery creates a proof collection moment. The SIA+ brief flags whether proof exists in the bank or needs to be requested.

07 Visual system

What every image must do

The visual system exists to sell emotion before copy does. Every image is assessed against the Cake-Card Wedge rule before being used. No staging for staging's sake.

Cake-Card Wedge rule (mandatory): Every product visual includes cake + note card + packaging context. Not cake alone. Not packaging alone. The wedge is what makes a photo feel like a gift — not a product shot.

Visual hierarchy (locked)

Priority 1 The cake — centred, clearly identifiable, good light. This is the product.
Priority 2 Gift context — hands, the open box, the note card, a reaction. This is the emotion.
Priority 3 Brand — logo, sticker, ribbon. Last. Never the first thing in frame.

Visual direction rules

Always shoot

  • Real over styled (candid > arranged)
  • Human over object (hands, faces, reactions)
  • Context over isolation (box open, gift given)
  • Delivery moment over studio flatlay

Never use

  • Empty aesthetic posts (no product, no context)
  • Over-styled shoots that feel commercial
  • Abstract or concept-led compositions
  • Stock photos or generic bakery imagery

Visual direction: product + price + CTA layout. Cake-Card Wedge.

12 Proof collection system

Active collection. Not passive hope.

Proof is not a content type you wait for — it's a system you run. The photo prompt script fires after every delivery. The Proof bank is the most valuable asset in this COS.

01

Delivery confirmed

Rider marks delivery complete. Triggers automated photo prompt (Script 03) via WhatsApp.

02

Photo prompt sent

"Hope it made them smile! Tag us @beqabakes for ₦500 off your next order."

03

Proof received

Photo or message received. Tagged in Notion "Proof bank" with date, product, buyer name.

04

Published Friday

Friday posts pull from Proof bank first. Reshare = ₦500 credit applied to buyer's account.

Bank target: Maintain a minimum of 4 unused proof items in the bank at all times. If the bank falls below 4, Friday posts create original Celebration content while urgently requesting new proof from recent buyers.

Write the first 3 weeks of SIA+ briefs in advance

9 briefs (3 weeks × 3 posts). Pre-writing removes the blank-page problem from weekly execution and turns content creation into a production task rather than a creative one. Assign mode, situation, product, and CTA for all 9 before shooting anything.

Week 1–2 — High leverage

WhatsApp automation setup

Scripts 01–04 are written. Wire them into WhatsApp Business or a tool like Wati / Interakt. Set triggers: Day 0 (delivery confirmed → Scripts 02 + 03 fire), Day 7 (Script 04 fires). This converts the retention loop from manual to passive — highest revenue-per-hour return on this list.

Week 2 — Proof system launch

Build the Proof bank to 10+ items

Send Script 03 manually to the last 20 delivery recipients today. The target is 10+ approved proof items in the bank before the first Friday post runs under this system. Without a stocked bank, Friday posts default to creating new content — which is the slower, harder path every week.

Produce 9 posts in a single shoot session using the 9 pre-written briefs. Each shot is pre-assigned: type, mode, product, Cake-Card Wedge composition. No improvising on the day. All images pass the checklist before the shoot ends. This is how 5–6 hrs/week becomes achievable.
